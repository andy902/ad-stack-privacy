Ad Stack — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 12, 2026

The short version: everything Ad Stack collects stays on your computer. Nothing is ever transmitted to us or to anyone else. There are no accounts, no servers, no analytics, and no data sales.

What Ad Stack does

Ad Stack is a browser extension with a single purpose: it blocks ads from appearing on web pages, saves each blocked ad's image and destination link on your device, and lets you review those ads later in a swipeable card stack.

What data is collected, and why

To power that one feature, Ad Stack records the following on your device only:

  • Ad images — the picture from each blocked ad, so it can be shown to you on a card.
  • Ad destination links — the advertiser's landing page URL, so "swipe right" can take you there.
  • The address and title of the page where each ad appeared — shown on the card as "seen on …". This is the only browsing-related data the extension touches, and it is recorded solely for ads it blocks.
  • Your settings — such as how many ads to keep and how often to save them.

Where the data lives

Captured ads are kept in the extension's local browser storage and, if you leave disk saving enabled, mirrored to a folder you own: Downloads\ad_stack\ — ordinary image files plus a human-readable ads.txt listing. You can open, copy, or delete these files at any time.

What is never done with your data

  • It is never transmitted off your device — there is no server to send it to.
  • It is never sold or transferred to third parties.
  • It is never used for advertising, profiling, or analytics.
  • It is never used to determine creditworthiness or for lending purposes.
  • No human other than you ever sees it.

Data retention and deletion

You control retention entirely. Ads beyond your configured limit are deleted automatically (oldest first). Swiping a card away deletes that ad and its saved files. "Delete all stored ads" in the settings removes everything at once. Uninstalling the extension removes its browser storage; any files already in your Downloads folder remain yours to keep or delete.

Permissions, in plain language

  • Access to websites — needed to find and hide ads on the pages you visit, and to read each ad's image and link.
  • Downloads — needed to write your ad archive into your Downloads folder and delete those files when you delete ads.
  • Storage, alarms, tabs — used to save your stack and settings, schedule batched disk writes, and open an advertiser's page when you swipe right.

Limited Use disclosure

Ad Stack's use of information received from Chrome APIs adheres to the Chrome Web Store User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted at this address with a new "last updated" date. Since the extension has no update mechanism for data practices — it simply has no data pipeline — material changes are not anticipated.
